    Typhoid

    Causes
    Often picked up in place where there are outbreaks,the bacteria passes out of the body in the stool and urine of people who are carrying the bacteria. Without careful hand-washing after going to the bathroom, the bacteria can move from the hands to objects or other people... It can also be gotten from raw fruits that haven't been washed or from places where water isn't treated to kill germs: which includes drinking water and using ice from untreated water.

    Symptoms
    Fever, chills, headache, fatigue, muscle pains, stomach ache, diarrhea and constipation and/or rashes.

    Treatments / Medication
    See a doctor as soon as possible and take the prescribed antibiotics as instructed. Drink a lot of water.

    Cough

    Causes
    A cough thta happens once in a while is common. It helps clear mucus from your lungs and prevents infection. But a cough that lasts for weeks is usually due to a health concern and might be either of the following reasons:

    Asthma- An asthma-related cough might be seasonal, often emerging after a respiratory infection. It can also worsen with exposure to cold air, certain chemicals, or fragrances. In a type of asthma called cough-variant asthma, coughing is the primary symptom.
    Postnasal drip- When your nose or sinuses produce extra mucus, it can drip down the back of your throat and cause you to cough. This condition also is called upper airway cough syndrome.
    Infections- A cough can last long after other symptoms of pneumonia, flu, a cold or another infection of the upper respiratory tract have gone away. A common cause of a chronic cough in adults — but one that often isn't recognized — is whooping cough.

    Symptoms
    A running nose, a feeling of liquid running down the back of your throat, also known as postnasal drip, sore throat, hoarseness, shortness of breath. In rare cases, coughing of blood.

    Treatments / Medication
    See a doctor if its a cough that lasts for weeks, especially one that brings up sputum or blood, disturbs your sleep, or affects school or work..

    Malaria

    Causes
    Malaria iscaused by a parasite. The parasite is transmitted to humans most commonly through mosquito bites. It could also be passed from moother to unborn child, through blood transfusions and by sharing needles used to inject drugs.

    Symptoms
    Feeling of discomfort, fever, chills, headache, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach pains, muscle/joint pain, fatigue, cough, rapid breathing, rapid heartbeat.

    Treatments / Medication
    See a doctor once symptoms persist or become severe.Take medication as instructed.

    Cold

    Causes
    It is a common illness that affects the nose and throat. Most often, its harmless but might not feel that way.Germs called viruses cause a common cold.

    Symptoms
    Generally feeling unwell, runny/stuffy nose, sore throat, cough, sneezing, mild headache.

    Treatments / Medication
    See a doctor if symptoms become severe, fever persists, shortness of breath or any of the other symptoms persist as it could be a symptom for something severe.

    Pneumonia

    Causes
    Pneumonia is an infection that causes inflammation in the air sacs of one or both lungs. These air sacs can fill with fluid or pus, leading to symptoms like coughing with mucus, fever and trouble breathing. Pneumonia can be caused by various organisms, including bacteria, viruses, and fungi..

    Symptoms
    Chest pain when you breathe or cough, Cough, Fever, Nausea, vomiting or diarrhea, shortness of breath and lower than normal body temperature (in adults older than age 65 and people with weak immune systems).

    Treatments / Medication
    Please see a doctor once symptoms persist or become severe. It is often adviced to get antibiotics, cough medicine and pain relievers

    Cholera

    Causes
    Cholera infection is caused by a bacteria whose harmful effects are caused by a toxin that the bacteria releases in the small intestine.The toxin causes the body to secrete enormous amounts of water, leading to diarrhea and a rapid loss of fluids and salts.Contaminated water supplies are the main source of cholera infection and the bateria can be found in contaminated water reserves, raw unwashed fruits and vegetables and places where grains like rice and millet left at room temperature can grow cholera bacteria.

    Symptoms
    Diarrhea, vomiting, fatigue, a dry mouth/extreme thirst, little to no urination, low blood pressure and an irregular heartbeat, dehydration and when it is more severe can cause electrolyte imbalance whose symptoms include muscle cramps and shock.

    Treatments / Medication
    Take preventive measures such as washing hands often and drinking purified water.Take antibiotics and meet a doctor if symptoms persist.

    Diarrhea

    Causes
    Diarrhea is a common condition characterized by frequent, loose, and watery stools.Sometimes, it's the only warning sign of a condition. Other times, it may come with symptoms like nausea, vomiting, stomach aches, or weight loss.It can be caused by viruses, bacteria and parasites, medicines, surgery or other digestive disorders.

    Symptoms
    stomach pain, bloating, vomiting, fever, frequent stooling, blood/mucus in stool.

    Treatments / Medication
    See a doctor if the stooling persists for more than two days or you're dehydrated or having bloody stools or terrible abdominal pains.

    Urinary Track Infection (UTI)

    Causes
    UTI is an infection in any part if the urinary system. It is normally caused by an infection of the urethra or the infection of the bladder.

    Symptoms
    Strong urge to urinate that doesn't go away, signs of blood in the urine, a burning feeling when urinating, urinating frequently and passing small amounts of urine, strong smelling urine, pelvic pain-in women especially in the center of the pelvis and around the area of the pubic bone.

    Treatments / Medication
    See a doctor once you notice any symptoms.

    Cold Sores

    Causes
    Cold sores, also known as fever blisters, are a common viral infection. They appear as small, fluid-filled blisters on or around the lips, often forming in clusters.It can be spread to the fac or genitals through contact such as kissin gand oral sex. Shared eating utensils, razors and towels can also transmit cold sores.

    Symptoms
    Tingling and itching, Blisters, Oozing and crusting, fever, headache, sore throat, painful gums, muscle aches, swollen lymph nodes.

    Treatments / Medication
    See a doctor if the cold sores don't heal within two weeks and the symptoms become severe.Also if the cold sores return often or you have a weak immune system, get in contact with a doctor.

    Herpes

    Causes
    Genital herpes is a common sexually transmitted infection (STI).Genital herpes can often be spread by skin-to-skin contact during sexual activity. Some people infected with the virus may have very mild symptoms or no symptoms. They can still able to spread the virus. Other people have pain, itching and sores around the genitals, anus or mouth.

    Symptoms
    Pain or itching around the genitals, small bumps or blisters around the genitals, anus or mouth, painful ulcers that form when blisters rupture and ooze or bleed, scabs that form as the ulcers heal, painful urination, discharge from the urethra, discharge from the vagina.You might also experience flu-like symptoms in the first outbreak such as fever, swollen nymph lodes in the groin, headaches and body aches.

    Treatments / Medication
    There is no cure for genital herpes. Symptoms often show up again after the first outbreak. Medicine can ease symptoms. It also lowers the risk of infecting others. Condoms can help prevent the spread of a genital herpes infection. However, do meet a doctor if you notice any symptoms.
